What is FIRST Global Challenge?

The FIRST Global Challenge (FGC) is an annual international robotics competition that unites high school students from around the world. Each year, teams from over 190 nations are challenged to work together to solve some of the world's most pressing issues, such as access to clean water, sustainable energy, or climate change, through robotics.

It's more than just building robots; FGC fosters cross-cultural communication, collaboration, and a passion for STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) among the next generation of global innovators. It empowers students to become leaders and problem-solvers in their communities and on the world stage.

This Year's Challenge: "Eco Equilibrium"

The 2025 FIRST Global Challenge, "Eco Equilibrium," focuses on **biodiversity and ecosystem restoration**. Teams are tasked with designing and building robots that can perform actions reflecting real-world efforts to protect endangered species, restore natural habitats, and manage critical resources.

The game challenges students to collect and sort "biodiversity units" (representing different species or resources) and mitigate "Barriers to Biodiversity" (represented by barrier balls). It symbolizes the delicate balance of ecosystems and the urgent need for innovative solutions to combat environmental degradation. By engaging with this theme, our team not only competes but also raises awareness about crucial environmental issues facing our planet.
