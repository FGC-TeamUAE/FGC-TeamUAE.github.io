TerraViva: Game Breakdown & Strategy

Understanding the field, mastering the challenge.

The Game Explained

The 2025 FIRST Global Challenge game, Eco Equilibrium, simulates a critical mission to restore global biodiversity. Teams must collaborate to perform various tasks on the playing field, representing actions like collecting and depositing biodiversity units and clearing environmental "barriers"".

Here is the official game explanation video for Eco Equilibrium.

Our Winning Strategy

Team UAE's strategy for Eco Equilibrium focuses on high-efficiency resource management and rapid, reliable endgame execution. Our primary goal is to **swiftly pass bio balls to human players** for efficient scoring, while our robot simultaneously excels at **mitigating barrier balls** to keep the field clear and maximize alliance points.

We are also confident in achieving **the best hanging** in the competition, a high-value endgame task. Our robot's design is specifically adapted to ensure a quick, stable, and repeatable climb, securing crucial points in the final moments of each match. Our strategic approach balances offensive capabilities with robust defense and a dominant endgame.

Robot Subsystem Breakdown

Intake

  • Compliant wheels at the front for gentle yet effective gripping.
  • Heat-treated orange tubing provides durability and optimized friction.
  • Surgical tubing drum positioned behind for efficient ball indexing.

Storage

  • Entire rear half side of the robot utilized for spacious storage.
  • Open roof design allows for quick loading and visual confirmation.
  • Capacity to store 4-6 bio balls (potentially up to 8 with optimization).

Launcher

  • None: Our strategy prioritizes passing and climbing over launching, simplifying the robot and reducing points of failure.

Climbing

  • Alternating gears and plastic brackets for a robust and reliable climbing mechanism.
  • Located above the surgical tubing drum, offset slightly to the back (center of the robot, above the intake).
  • Designed for optimal balance and stability during ascent.

Barrier Ball Mitigation

  • Rear wall features two extrusion "arms" with compliant wheels on the insides.
  • Servos control movement from upright (storage) to barrier ball height (active mitigation).
  • Cardboard / PEI sheet between wheels, also servo-mounted, keeps bio balls in but can flip out for barrier ball intake.
  • Whole mechanism folds backward without opening PEI sheet to expand bio ball storage.
  • Small ledge at the bottom to securely hold barrier balls during transport.

Generator Spinner

  • Minor subsystem located on the side of the robot.
  • Utilizes a servo with gearing for precise and efficient rotation of the generator.

Drivetrain

  • X-Drive: Provides superior maneuverability, allowing for omnidirectional movement and precise positioning on the field.
  • Enables quick adjustments and evasion, crucial for navigating crowded areas and optimizing ball transfer.
